South Korea Wallz

Back in the Pow! Wow! days (before the Wallz makeover), I took the lead in designing murals for a festival that spanned across 50 locations. Pow! Wow! South Korea reached out, and together we infused traditional Korean vibes into the festival. We wanted an off shade of white to be our primary color. White, representing purity and a 'clean state of mind without greed,' mirrored the historical connection with the Korean people, once known as "Baeguiminjok (the white-clad people)." This choice aligns seamlessly with Pow! Wow!'s non-profit mission to uplift city neighborhoods.

South Korea Wallz Program

I took on the responsibility of designing the program, Pow! Wow! Passport, for the mural event. Choosing a natural aesthetic, I incorporated elements to represent traditional Korea. Utilizing a Korean typeface, I flipped and integrated the word 'passport' to English to give it more of a Korean essence. The interior paper closely resembled traditional Korean Hanji paper, adding some cultural authenticity to the design.

South Korea Wallz Social

We collaborated with a diverse group of local and international artists for the event. To introduce each artist effectively on social media, I aimed to provide a clear representation of their work. The images initially provided showcased past murals, but the busy backgrounds detracted from the artists' work. Taking a focused approach, I removed distracting elements, allowing a spotlight on each artist's work. To enhance the traditional feel, I incorporated an image of Hanji in the background, also contributing to a clear visual of the artists' work.
